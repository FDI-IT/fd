INVENTORY

The handling of allergen containing raw materials is critical to eliminate the possibility of contamination. Please answer the following questions;

  1. When receiving a raw material how do you know if it is an allergen?
  2. What steps best describes what do you take to make sure there is no cross contamination of that allergen?
    • A sample is taken of the raw material and is sent up to QC
    • The individual item or palette is then clearly marked and the receiving sticker is attached. It is then placed into an area in the plant marked with a magnet sign or signs indicating the allergen types. If there is more than one allergen contained in the product it is either separated into a different spot or put lower than any of the allergens contained.
    • All of the above
    • The raw material is not used in a batch until the price is confirmed.
  3. What best describes the procedure for inventory?
    • If an allergen example soy is next to a wheat allergen they must be at least 20 feet away from each other.
    • If there must be storage above, it must be the same allergen or contain at least one of multiple allergens below.
    • Fish allergens must be kept near shellfish allergens.
    • The magnetic signs have to appear on the top shelf only.
